## In My Own Words

**What is the CIA Triad, in your own words?**
The CIA Triad is the foundation of cybersecurity because it helps protect information in three important ways. Confidentiality means that only authorized people can access sensitive data. Integrity ensures that information remains accurate and has not been changed or tampered with. Availability means that systems and data are accessible when authorized users need them. All three work together to keep information secure and reliable.

**Which cybersecurity career path interests you most right now, and why?**
I'm most interested in GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance), cloud security, and PKI (Public Key Infrastructure). My background in education and running a tax business has given me experience following regulations, handling sensitive information, and paying close attention to detail, which aligns well with GRC. I'm also interested in cloud security because it's a rapidly growing area of cybersecurity. PKI has especially caught my attention because there is a strong demand for professionals with those skills, and I enjoy learning about technologies that play an important role in protecting organizations.

**One term I want to remember from this week, and why:**
One term I want to remember is the STAR Method. Although it wasn't part of the Cyber Foundations lessons, it was introduced during our Linkedin workshop. STAR stands for Situation, Task, Action, and Result, and it's a way to explain your experience by focusing on the problem you faced, the actions you took, and the outcome you achieved. I want to become more comfortable using this method because it will help me write stronger résumé bullet points and answer interview questions more effectively.
